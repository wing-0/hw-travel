▸japan•fushimi inari taisha◂

꒰⋮ Intro ⋮꒱

The Fushimi Inari Taisha is recognized as the head shrine of all Shinto shrines, dedicated to Inari Okami, the god of rice harvest. It was built during the Nara Period in 711 A.D, and is a designated Important Cultural Property in Japan. The main shrine of the complex houses five pillars which represent the great values of Inari. From its establishment up to the present, people come here to worship and pray for good harvest, safety, wealth, marriage, and many other kinds of wishes. At the temple, you’ll notice the fox statues scattered around. These statues are called kitsune, which is said to be Inari’s messengers, a symbolism of the kami (deities). You’ll often see these statues holding a ball or a scroll which symbolizes the message to be sent from the earth or from the celestial sphere.
